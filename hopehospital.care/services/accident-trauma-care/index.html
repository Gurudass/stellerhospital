Accident and trauma care is a specialized field of medicine at Hope Hospitals that focuses on treating individuals who have suffered injuries or illnesses as a result of an accident or traumatic event. The goal of accident and trauma care is to provide prompt, effective, and comprehensive care to patients, with the aim of minimizing the negative impact of the injury or illness and maximizing patient recovery. The field of accident and trauma care is interdisciplinary, involving a range of medical professionals, including emergency medical technicians, paramedics, nurses, and physicians, who work together to provide patients with the best possible care.

Accident and trauma care services at Hope Hospitals typically involve the use of advanced medical equipment and techniques, such as emergency medical services, surgery, pain management, rehabilitation, and mental health support. Patients may be treated in a variety of settings, including emergency departments, trauma centres, rehabilitation facilities, and outpatient clinics. The treatment approach in accident and trauma care is highly individualized and may include a range of techniques, such as wound care and dressing, medications, physical therapy, occupational therapy, and counselling.

Hope Hospital provides comprehensive care for individuals who have sustained injuries from accidents and traumatic events,

Some of the conditions treated at Hope Hospital in its Accident & Trauma care unit, includes

Fractures – Bone fractures from a traumatic event can be treated with surgery or casts.

Lacerations – Deep cuts from sharp objects can be treated with stitches or skin glue.

Burns – Burns from fire, chemicals or electricity can be treated with pain management, wound care, and skin grafts.

Concussions -Traumatic brain injuries can cause temporary changes in brain function and require monitoring and rehabilitation.

Spinal Injuries – Injuries to the spine can cause temporary or permanent paralysis and require specialized care.

Soft Tissue Injuries – Strains and sprains from overexertion or accidents can be treated with physical therapy, pain management, and bracing.

Internal Injuries – Injuries to internal organs from blunt force trauma can be treated with surgery and other medical interventions.

Dislocations – Dislocated joints can be treated with reductions, splints or casts.

Amputations – Traumatic amputations can require rehabilitation, prosthetics and psychological support.

Eye Injuries – Eye injuries from blunt force, chemicals or sharp objects can be treated with surgery or other medical interventions to prevent vision loss.

Hope Hospital provides a wide range of treatment options for Accident and Trauma patients

Emergency Department

The emergency department is equipped with state-of-the-art facilities and staffed by experienced medical professionals who provide prompt and effective treatment for accidental injuries and trauma. The team includes emergency medicine physicians, nurses, and support staff who are trained to handle a variety of medical emergencies.

Surgical Interventions

In cases of severe injuries, surgical intervention may be necessary. Hope Hospital has a team of highly skilled and experienced surgeons who specialize in a range of procedures, including orthopaedic surgery, neurosurgery, and plastic surgery, among others.

Intensive Care Unit

Patients with life-threatening injuries or conditions may require close monitoring and support in an intensive care unit (ICU). The ICU at Hope Hospital is equipped with advanced monitoring equipment and staffed by specially trained ICU nurses and physicians.

Radiology and Imaging Services

Accurate diagnosis of traumatic injuries often requires the use of imaging techniques such as X-rays, CT scans, and MRI scans. Hope Hospital has a modern radiology department that provides high-quality imaging services to support the diagnosis and treatment of traumatic injuries.

Physical Rehabilitation

Physical rehabilitation is an important aspect of the recovery process for many patients who have suffered injuries as a result of an accident or trauma. Hope Hospital has a team of physical therapists who work with patients to develop customized rehabilitation programs to help them regain strength, flexibility, and mobility.

Trauma Counselling and Support

Traumatic events can have a significant psychological impact, and Hope Hospital recognizes the importance of providing counselling and support to patients and their families. The hospital offers a range of mental health services, including trauma counselling, to help patients and their families cope with the aftermath of a traumatic event.

What is an accident and trauma care centre?

A medical facility specializing in the immediate treatment of individuals with injuries from accidents or other traumatic events.

 

What kind of injuries does an accident and trauma centre treat?

Injuries can range from broken bones, head injuries, burns, and internal injuries to more severe injuries such as severe bleeding and spinal cord injuries.

 

What are the steps to follow after an accident?

Call emergency services, provide first aid, if possible, do not move the injured person unless necessary, wait for medical help to arrive, and get the necessary information.

 

How long does it take for an accident and trauma centre to stabilize a patient?

It depends on the severity of the injury and the individual’s condition, but the goal is to stabilize the patient as quickly as possible.

 

What should I do if someone has a head injury?

Call emergency services, keep the person still, monitor breathing and pulse, and apply pressure to any visible wounds.

 

How does the Emergency Department handle accident cases?

The staffs at the Emergency Department are highly-skilled professionals who have received specific training in dealing with accident cases and many other types of emergency medical situations. This department plays the most important role in saving the lives that come through its doors.

Accident cases are critical and often require immediate medical attention. Accident victims are brought to the Emergency Department via the ambulance / self and are given immediate first aid treatment. If the condition of the patient is serious, specialised doctors are brought onto the scene to handle the patient.

 

Is it necessary to go to an accident and trauma centre after an injury?

It depends on the severity of the injury, but it is always advisable to seek medical attention after any injury.

 

What equipment and resources are available at an accident and trauma centre?

Advanced equipment such as CT scans, X-rays, and ventilators, as well as trained medical personnel.
